That the DCCG adopt weighted voting for Operational decisions — the "actual doings" of the group. Constitutional and Procedural votes stay one-member-one-vote, everybody equal. This motion changes how we vote only on Operational matters, and works like this:
The boundary comes first. No decision of the group can override a person's consent over their own land, home, or body. Where a decision lands on one member that way, it needs their consent — that isn't put to a vote, weighted or otherwise, and can't be overturned by one.
Then the toggle. Every Operational motion starts with one question: does this affect the whole community, or does it land unevenly? If it affects everyone alike, it's one member, one vote. Weighting only comes into play when a decision clearly falls on some members more than others — and when it does, the person proposing it says so plainly, on the motion, where anyone can see and challenge it.
Then, only where weighted, the weights. The say leans along whichever of three lines fits:

Place — those nearest to, or most affected by, where the decision lands.
Commitment — those pledging time, labour or resources to this motion.
Contribution — those with a track record of having delivered in the past.

Weighting reflects where a decision falls — never how long someone has lived here.
One more thing about big decisions. Some can't be undone once made. Those pass on a higher bar than decisions we can revisit later.
